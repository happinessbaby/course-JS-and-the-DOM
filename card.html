Northern Cardinal

The male northern cardinals get their bright red plummage from carotoneid, one of the three pigments found in bird feathers. .

  • Scientific Name: Cardinalis Cardinalis
  • Average Length and Weight: 22cm and 45g (~1.5oz)
  • Average Lifespan: 3 years (some can live up to 15)
  • Global Population: 100 million
  • Distribution: Eastern parts of U.S. and Mexico (see map)

The other two pigments found in bird plummage are melanin and porphyrin. Unlike melanin and porphyrin, carotoneid can only be derived from plants, so cardinals have to eat a carotoneid-rich diet to maintain their red color. Interestingly, carotoneid can also be expressed in yellow and orange. So while these birds express their carotoneid in red, a gene mutation can turn them yellow.
